---
title: "How do I cite Weights & Biases in academic papers?"
keywords: ["Academic"]
---

To cite Weights & Biases in academic papers, use the whitepaper by Lukas Biewald (the founder of Weights & Biases):

> Biewald, L. (2020). *Experiment Tracking with Weights and Biases*. Weights & Biases. Software available from [wandb.com](https://www.wandb.com/).

The following BibTeX entry is a commonly used format:

```bibtex
@misc{wandb,
  title = {Experiment Tracking with Weights and Biases},
  year = {2020},
  note = {Software available from wandb.com},
  url = {https://www.wandb.com/},
  author = {Biewald, Lukas},
}
```

If you use a specific W&B product (for example, Weave, Sweeps, or Registry), also link to the relevant documentation section in your paper so readers can reproduce your workflow.
